Digital Breath Test Screening Data Exploration

This Shiny App is for exploring and performing analysis on collected breath-test screening data for 2014. 
The data are recorded by digital breath testing devices from the Police Authorities in England and Wales.

References:
Source link to DATA.GOV.UK, Road Safety Data

* To access the raw data, expand the "2014 Data Resources" node, then search for "2014 Road Safety - digital Breath Test Data 2014".  
The raw dataset is available for download from above url link. 
 

Taxonomy of variables in the Digital Breath Test Raw Dataset: 

ITEM DESCRIPTION  
Reason Reason for administering digital breath test by police
Month Month when roadside breath test was conducted (e.g. Jan, Feb, Mar, Apr etc.)
Year Year when roadside breath test was conducted (e.g. 2009, 2010). This field is removed during data preparation since all data were from year 2014.
WeekType Day type when roadside breath test was conducted (e.g. weekend, weekday)
TimeBand Time period when breath test was conducted, in 4-hourly bands (e.g. 12am-4am, 4am-8am, 8am-12pm etc.)
BreathAlcoholLevel(microg/100ml) Breath test reading from digital breath meter used by police officer (micrograms of alcohol per 100 milliletres of breath)
AgeBand Age group (in years) of person administered the roadside breath test (e.g. 16-19, 20-24, 25-29, 30-39 etc.)
Gender Gender of person administered the roadside breath test (e.g. male, female)

 

Additional variables in dataset 
    
In this Shiny App, additional custom variables were added to raw dataset to facilitate easier reporting by aggregation/summation grouping.  

The additional variables are as follow:

ITEM DESCRIPTION  
Quarter A variable to group records by quarters. To faciliate easier record grouping during record summation/aggregation function.
Test.Performed                           A dummy variable to faciliate total breath test record count during record summation/aggregation function.

Alcohol.Detected                      

 A variable to indicate whether alcohol were detected during the breath test. To facilitate positive/negative test record count during  segregation /summation.
